Infant Mortality Rates Across U.S. Counties

While the average infant mortality rate in America has been declining over the last decade and is around 5.8 per 1,000 births, it varies greatly in different counties, ranging from less than 5 to over 19 per 1,000.


Infant mortality rates are highest among African Americans and are rising with the size of the black population, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention data for 2011-2015. Counties in Mississippi, Alabama and Georgia where over 50 percent of popualation is black, the infant mortality rates reach up to 20 per 1,000, while it is around 2 per 1,000 in counties with less than 1 percent of black population.


American Latinos show the opposite trend. The larger the hispanic population, the lower the infant mortality rate. Counties with over 50 percent of Latino population have infant mortality rates around 5 per 1,000.


Counties with 5 percent or lower of households in poverty, have 5 and lower infant mortality rates per 1,000. Counties with about 30 percent of households in poverty have infant mortality rates of approximately 10 per 1,000. The rate climbs to 20 per 1,000 in households with the poverty level around 40 percent or slightly higher.
